# Body Signals — Competitive Brief

**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-11

---

## Positioning Statement

Body Signals is the evidence-transparent, no-affiliate-bias health reference for Canadian and US readers who want to understand what their body is telling them — not be sold supplements, frightened into clicks, or buried in medical jargon. We rate every claim on an A–F evidence scale, name our sources, and show what we don't know alongside what we do.

## Positioning Axes

### 1. Evidence Transparency
**Body Signals:** A–F ratings on every claim, named sources, hand-authored per record.
**WebMD/Healthline:** "Medically reviewed" badges with no per-claim grading.
**Mayo Clinic:** Institutional authority but no intervention-level evidence grading.

### 2. No Affiliate Bias
**Body Signals:** Zero advertising, zero affiliate links, zero supplement sales.
**Healthline:** Revenue from affiliate links (supplements, products).
**WebMD:** Revenue from pharmaceutical advertising and sponsored content.

### 3. Canadian / US Co-Branded
**Body Signals:** Canadian spelling, Health Canada + FDA, provincial plans + Medicare/Medicaid, jurisdiction-correct crisis resources.
**US competitors:** US-only framing. Canadian readers must mentally translate.
**Canadian competitors:** Province-specific or mental-health-only. US readers not served.

### 4. Tactical Topic Depth
**Body Signals:** 20 sleep interventions with protocols, 18 ADHD systems with difficulty/impact ratings, 3 movement programs with week-by-week progressions.
**Competitors:** Typically condition-only coverage. No structured intervention libraries with evidence grading.

### 5. Quality Over Volume
**Body Signals:** 113 hand-authored pages. Every record individually reviewed.
**Healthline:** Thousands of articles, SEO-scaled, variable depth.
**WebMD:** Massive library but quality varies; many pages are thin.

### 6. What Body Signals Does NOT Do
This is as important as what it does:
- **No symptom checker with diagnosis** — we provide information, not differential diagnosis.
- **No personalisation** — no accounts, no tailored recommendations, no data collection.
- **No supplement content marketing** — supplements appear only as interventions within conditions, evidence-rated like everything else.
- **No user-generated content** — no comments, no forums, no community features.

## Key Takeaway

Body Signals occupies a positioning gap that no current competitor fills: **evidence-transparent, no-affiliate-bias, Canadian AND US co-branded, with tactical topic depth alongside clinical condition coverage.** The Canadian competitors are too narrow (province-specific or MH-only). The US competitors are too broad and ad-funded. Body Signals is neither — it's a focused, hand-authored reference that treats evidence transparency as the product, not marketing.
